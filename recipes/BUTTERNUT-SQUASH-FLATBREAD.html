BUTTERNUT SQUASH FLATBREAD

Description

Fallâ€™s best flavors come together in this delicious recipe for Butternut Squash Flatbread. This flatbread can be served as an appetizer or as the main dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash (halved lengthwise, seeds removed)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (plus extra for brushing onto flatbread)
  • 50 grams goat cheese (divided)
  • 1/4 cup orange juice (freshly squeezed or store-bought)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (freshly ground)
  • 1 large store-bought flatbreads
  • 1/2 cup shallots (thinly sliced)
  • 50 grams thinly sliced prosciutto torn into strips
  • 40 grams parmesan cheese (freshly grated)
  • 3 green onions (thinly sliced)
  • drizzle of honey

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Place squash halves cut side up on a rimmed baking sheet and drizzle with 1 tablespoon olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  2. Bake squash for 50-60 minutes until brown and fork-tender. When the roasted squash is cool enough to handle, scoop the pulp into a large bowl.
  3. With an electric hand mixer blend in goat cheese, orange juice, sage, thyme, and nutmeg to the squash. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  4. Increase oven heat to 425 degrees F. Place flatbread onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and brush the tops with a thin coating of olive oil. Bake 3-5 minutes to crisp the crust (skip this if you prefer a chewier crust).
  5. Remove flatbread from oven and evenly spread on the squash mixture.
    Top with prosciutto, shallots, crumbles of remaining goat cheese, and sage leaves. Sprinkle with freshly grated parmesan.
  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes until edges are crisp.
  7. Finish with fresh cracked black pepper, additional parmesan, green onions and drizzle of honey. Enjoy!
